Josephson Junction

An oxide barrier between two metals across which electrons may be forced to tunnel. When immersed in liquid helium, a circuit containing such a junction is able to switch at very high speeds with low power dissipation. It thus avoids the heat-transfer problems that arise from silicon VLSI devices and is one way to attain superconductivity.
