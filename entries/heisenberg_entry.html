Heisenberg, Werner

Werner Heisenberg is best known as a founder of quantum mechanics and the uncertainty principle in quantum theory. He is also known for his controversial role as a leader of Germany's nuclear fission research during World War II. After the war he was active in elementary particle physics and West German science policy.
