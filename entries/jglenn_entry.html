Glenn, Jerome C..

Glenn co-founded and directs the Millennium Project and invented the "Futures Wheel" forecasting technique and Futuristic Curriculum Development. He is the executive director of the American Council for the United Nations University; an international development consultant; and a prolific author of future-oriented articles. He was the deputy director, PfP International involved in micro-credit, national strategic planning, institutional design, training, and evaluation in economic development in Africa, Middle East, Asia, the Caribbean, and Latin America, founded CARINET the computer network in 1983 (now owned by CGNET), and has consulted for corporations, USAID and its contractors, World Bank, UNDP, UNU, UNESCO, US/EPA, DOE, and several governments.
